According to the reported benchmarks, VibeThinker-3B gets surprisingly close to much larger coding and reasoning systems.

What I found most interesting is that the model builds on the older Qwen2.5-Coder-3B stack. Based on the model card, VibeThinker-3B has 3.09B parameters and uses a Qwen2-style backbone.

So, this is a pretty clear example of how much can come from good data curation and post-training.

The technical report is worth reading because it gives a detailed look at the post-training pipeline. The important pieces are:

  1. high-signal synthetic math and code data with verifiable solutions
  2. multiple reasoning paths per answer, followed by aggressive filtering
  3. two-stage supervised finetuning, first broad coverage and then harder long-reasoning examples
  4. checkpoint selection by target accuracy rather than validation loss alone
  5. MGPO, a GRPO-style RLVR method that emphasizes samples that are neither too easy nor too hard
  6. single-stage 64k-context RL, instead of progressive context expansion
  7. domain RL ordered as math, then code, then STEM
  8. a later stage that rewards shorter correct trajectories
  9. offline self-distillation from filtered Math, Code, and STEM RL trajectories
  10. final instruction RL using rule-based validators and rubric-based reward models

They do not share the exact GPU hours for this project. Based on numbers from the earlier VibeThinker-1.5B report, my rough guess would be that this cost on the order of $25k to $60k. That is still a lot of money, but it is not millions.

One caveat is that as of June 17, 2026, the model is very new, and the benchmarks could still be too good to be true. It needs practical use over the next few days to see whether the VibeThinker results check out in practice. But as a first impression, this is a strong small-model result and a useful post-training write-up.

根据已公布的基准测试结果，VibeThinker-3B 的表现令人惊讶地接近许多规模大得多的编程与推理系统。

最让我感兴趣的是，该模型基于较早期的 Qwen2.5-Coder-3B 技术栈构建。根据模型卡片信息，VibeThinker-3B 拥有 30.9 亿参数，并采用了 Qwen2 风格的骨干网络。

因此，这清晰地展示了优质数据整理与后训练能带来多大的提升。

这份技术报告值得一读，因为它详细介绍了后训练流程。其中的关键环节包括：

  1. 使用带有可验证解决方案的高信号合成数学与代码数据
  2. 每个答案生成多条推理路径，随后进行严格筛选
  3. 两阶段监督微调：第一阶段追求广泛覆盖，第二阶段聚焦更难的长推理示例
  4. 依据目标准确率（而非仅凭验证损失）选择检查点
  5. MGPO：一种 GRPO 风格的 RLVR 方法，侧重于那些既不太简单也不太难的样本
  6. 单阶段 64k 上下文强化学习，而非渐进式上下文扩展
  7. 领域强化学习的顺序为：先数学，再编程，最后 STEM
  8. 后期阶段奖励更短的正确推理轨迹
  9. 基于筛选后的数学、编程和 STEM 强化学习轨迹进行离线自我蒸馏
  10. 最终使用基于规则的验证器和基于评分标准的奖励模型进行指令强化学习

他们并未透露该项目的确切 GPU 使用时长。根据早期 VibeThinker-1.5B 报告中的数据，我粗略估计成本大约在 2.5 万到 6 万美元之间。这仍然是一笔不小的开支，但远未达到数百万美元的量级。

需要注意的是，截至 2026 年 6 月 17 日，该模型非常新，其基准测试结果可能好得令人难以置信。未来几天需要通过实际应用来检验 VibeThinker 的结果是否在实践中站得住脚。但就第一印象而言，这是一个出色的小模型成果，也是一份有价值的后训练技术总结。
